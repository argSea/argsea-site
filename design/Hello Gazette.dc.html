Giant Monolith Quietly Dismantled; the Paper Never Misses a Day
29 small, well-behaved services now stand where one enormous application once feared nothing. Readers, by design, noticed nothing at all.
By the Keeper reporting from inside the cluster · est. 2024, ongoing
The old system was one enormous application that did everything and feared nothing, least of all the keeper's weekends. It was taken apart piece by piece: a message queue in the middle, 29 services around it, Kubernetes herding the containers.
The success metric was silence. No reader ever noticed a thing. The paper published every single day while the engine was swapped mid-flight. The old stack was decommissioned in 2025, capability by capability, until one night there was nothing left to turn off.
Moral: the best migrations are the boring ones.
